Sure! Let me walk you through the entire methodology transparently.

1. 📥 Data Collection

Used a Python library called google-play-scraper which pulls reviews directly from the Play Store API. For each app, I fetched the 300 most recent reviews sorted by newest first, from India (country='in', lang='en').

Each review came with:

  • Text (the comment)
  • Score (1–5 stars)
  • Date

2. 🗂️ Category Assignment (Rule-Based Keyword Matching)

This is the most important part to understand. I did not use AI to categorize — I used a keyword matching algorithm. Each category had a list of trigger words:

CategoryKeywords used
Delivery & Logisticsdeliver, courier, ship, track, delay, late, arrived…
Returns & Refundsreturn, refund, exchange, replace, money back…
App Experienceapp, crash, bug, load, slow, glitch, filter, search…
Customer Supportsupport, service, agent, help, customer care…
Product Qualityquality, fabric, material, size, fit, fake, authentic…
Pricing & Offersprice, discount, offer, coupon, sale, value…
Packagingpackag, box, wrap, damage, torn, seal…
Payment & Checkoutpayment, pay, checkout, wallet, upi, card, failed…
Order Managementorder, wrong item, missing, cancel, wrong product…
Overall Satisfactiongood, great, love, best, worst, bad, excellent…

For each review, it counted how many keywords from each category matched. The category with the highest match score won. If nothing matched, it defaulted to Overall Satisfaction.

3. 😊 Sentiment Assignment (Star Rating + Keywords)

Sentiment was determined by a combination of star rating and keyword presence:

  • ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4–5 stars) → Positive
  • ⭐⭐ (1–2 stars) → Negative
  • ⭐⭐⭐ (3 stars) → checked for positive/negative words to decide; defaulted to Neutral if tied

⚠️ Known Limitations to Keep in Mind

  1. "Overall Satisfaction" is over-represented — Short reviews like "nice", "good", "worst app" don't contain specific keywords, so they fall into this bucket. That's why it's 177/181 for both apps.
  2. Keyword matching can misfire — A review saying "the app crashed while I was tracking my order" would match both App Experience and Delivery. It gets assigned to whichever has more keyword hits, which may not always be accurate.
  3. English only — Reviews in Hindi, Tamil, or other languages were fetched but likely miscategorized since the keywords are all English.
  4. Sentiment can conflict with text — A 5-star review saying "worst packaging ever but great clothes" would be marked Positive because of the star rating.
